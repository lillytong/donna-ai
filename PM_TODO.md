**The pain it removes.** The operator opens an issue mid-call ("storage rate too low, push to market"). **1. What Donna recommends in v1 — one recommendation object, not three features.**

v1 produces a single grounded recommendation per opened issue = **{short cited rationale + recommended landing (`recommended_position`) + drafted clause language (`donna_counter_language`)}**. Framed on DD-14 rule 4's reasonableness spectrum (favorable-but-fair → aggressive → deal-breaking) with a fallback ladder (ask / settle / floor) — the field already anticipates this and it is what makes the card briefable to a principal.

- **Propose vs. counter is the same engine with a different input field, not two builds** (collapse-before-build). Operator-raised issue → `our_position` populated → Donna recommends a landing + drafts *our* clause language. Counterparty-raised / Mode-B issue → `their_position` populated → Donna recommends a *counter* + drafts counter-language. The engine reads {clause text + our/their positions + status} and emits {landing + language + rationale}; which fields are populated decides the stance. One pass.
- **Why both reasoning AND language, as a unit:** a landing with no drafted clause leaves the hardest part (the words) undone → fails effort-saved; drafted words with no rationale are an assertion she can't defend to her principal → fails §2.4. They ship together or not at all. (The conservative cut — reasoning-only first — is **Open Q1**, a real risk call for Lilly.)
